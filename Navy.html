Indian Naval Aviation

About

The role of the Indian Naval Aviation is to protect India's maritime interests and also support the Indian Air Force in the peninsular region. The main wing of the Indian Naval Aviation are the India's 2 Aircraft Carriers INS Vikrant and INS Vikramaditya, which act as Force Multipliers. The Indian Navy also provides security to the merchant ships from the pirates present in the Indian Ocean. The Indian Navy provides both men and women, a great oppurtunity to serve the nation. The Indian naval aviators are at par with US Navy's Top Gun

Eligibility Conditions

  • Age: Candidates must not be older than 23 years.
  • Education:Minimum 10+2 qualification with Physics and Mathematics and must hold a graduation degree from a recognized university.
  • Nationality:Must be a citizen of India.
  • Physical Standards:As per Indian Navy standards.
  • Fitness:Candidates must be medically fit as per Navy standards.

Selection Procedure

  • A written exam conducted by the UPSC.
  • The exam is followed by an SSB (Services Selection Board) Interview.
  • The selected candidates undergo a full-body medical check-up.
  • The merit list is prepared based on written and SSB marks.
  • Joining letters are issued based on the vacancies and merit.
